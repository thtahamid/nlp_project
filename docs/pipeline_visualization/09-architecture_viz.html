01 quantization · what each float actually looks like

Bit widths, byte costs, and why nf4 is the 4× trick

02 LoRA rank-32 · the matrix stuffs

Why ~0.6% of parameters carries the finetune

LoRA replaces a full weight update ΔW with a low-rank factorization ΔW = B·A, where B ∈ ℝd×r and A ∈ ℝr×k. With r=32 on a 3584×3584 projection, you replace 12.8 M trainable params with 229 K — a 56× reduction per layer. The base W stays frozen (and 4-bit quantised).

Scaling up rank: LoRA params scale linearly in r. r=8 → 20.2 M · r=16 → 40.3 M · r=32 → 80.7 M · r=64 → 161 M · r=128 → 322 M. Rank 32 is the max that fits on 24 GB A10G with batch=1 + grad-ckpt on 7B-VL; rank 64 OOMs.

04 qwen3.5-4b · the consumer-gpu tier

Same hybrid architecture, downscaled

Qwen3.5-4B is the small-tier sibling of 9B. Same Gated-DeltaNet hybrid, smaller hidden size, fewer FFN params, vocab/embedding are identical (both tiers share the 248 k BPE tokenizer, so embedding tables scale only with hidden size). The 4B version introduces tie_word_embeddings=true — the LM head reuses the embedding matrix, saving ~650 M params.

The hybrid layer pattern · both 4B and 9B

Why hybrid: linear attention (Gated DeltaNet) is O(n) in sequence length → enables 256 K native context. A sparse sprinkling of full softmax attention (every 4th layer) preserves the expressivity needed for in-context learning. The DeltaNet layers don't round-trip cleanly through bnb 4-bit, which is why QLoRA is unstable on Qwen3.5 — use bf16 LoRA.

The decisive win is item 3 vs item 2: the full 7 B base stays in 4-bit (4.15 GB, frozen), only the 80.7 M LoRA params get bf16 weights + bf16 gradients + fp32 Adam state (~0.32 + 0.32 + 0.64 ≈ 1.3 GB). 07 note · there is no qwen3.5-omni (yet)

Omni line naming as of 2026-04-18: the current open-weight Omni family is Qwen3-Omni-30B-A3B (Sep 2025, arXiv 2509.17765) in three post-training variants — Instruct, Thinking, and Captioner. There is no Qwen3.5-Omni release; Qwen3.5 itself is the natively-VL text+image line, while Omni (audio + video in, speech out) stays under the Qwen3-Omni-* branding. Smaller dense Omni (3B / 7B) only exists in the earlier Qwen2.5-Omni generation.
